How we evaluate listings

Prefab homes sold online are often closer to a “structure + interior shell” than a turnkey Canadian home. We prioritize practical planning:

Documentation

  • Spec clarity (dimensions, materials, assembly method)
  • Electrical approvals (CSA/UL/ETL where applicable)
  • Drawings or manuals where available

Canada install reality

  • Foundation and frost depth planning
  • Insulation and air sealing expectations
  • Delivery access and staging area requirements

Costs beyond the listing

  • Permits, engineering (if needed), inspections
  • Electrical, plumbing, HVAC, winterization
  • Site prep, crane/forklift (if needed), finishing

Red flags

  • Unclear certifications or missing specs
  • “Too good to be true” promises without documentation
  • Photos that don’t match stated dimensions or contents

Prices, availability, shipping terms, and included components can change without notice. When possible, we note what appears included, but you should always confirm the final details on the seller/retailer page before purchase.

Buyer verification checklist

Before purchasing any prefab home or related product, you should:

  • Confirm zoning, setbacks, and occupancy rules with your municipality
  • Request documentation from the seller (drawings, certifications, specs)
  • Verify electrical approvals (CSA/UL/ETL where applicable)
  • Confirm insulation levels and climate suitability for your region
  • Understand delivery access, foundation, and installation requirements
  • Budget for utilities, winterization, permits, and inspections
