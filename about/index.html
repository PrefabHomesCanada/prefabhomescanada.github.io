Our mission

Help Canadian buyers make better decisions by reducing surprises and highlighting what matters after checkout.

We translate “listing language” into real planning

Many online prefab listings look turnkey, but arrive as a structure or interior shell. We focus on the practical gap between what you see in photos and what you need to live comfortably in Canada.

We prioritize clarity over hype

If insulation, windows, electrical approvals, or snow-load assumptions aren’t clearly stated, we treat them as unknown. Our job is to flag uncertainty, not to “sell a dream.”

What we publish

PrefabHomesCanada publishes buyer guides, checklists, and planning sections that cover the parts most people learn the hard way.

Who it’s for

We write for buyers who want a realistic view of prefab projects in Canada.

Best fit

  • People comparing online prefab listings and want fewer surprises
  • Buyers planning cabins, guest suites, studios, or phased builds
  • Anyone who wants checklists before contacting sellers

Not a fit

  • Buyers expecting a fully code-approved Canadian home “out of the box”
  • Anyone looking for legal, engineering, or permit approvals from a website
  • People who want “one price” that applies to every municipality

Editorial principles

These rules keep our guides consistent, even when listings change.

1) Specs beat adjectives

If a listing says “luxury” or “winter ready” but doesn’t provide measurable specs, we treat it as a claim, not a fact.

2) Unknown stays unknown

If insulation values, window specs, approvals, or load assumptions aren’t clearly stated, we label them as not specified.

3) Installed cost matters

We emphasize site work, permits, utilities, and winterization because they often drive the “real budget.”

4) Canada-first reality

Cold weather, snow load, and municipal rules change the game. Our guides assume Canadian constraints, not generic marketing.
